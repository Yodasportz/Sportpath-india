📚 Class 9-10: the decision window
Ages 14-16 is when sport-career paths lock in. Miss this window and your options shrink — every year after Class 10 costs something. Start here if you're in Class 8/9/10 right now.

If you're in Class 9-10: your next 3 moves

1. Register with the CBSE/ICSE Sports Board + district federation
CBSE accepts Physical Education as a main Class 11-12 subject since 2024. Register with your district sports office and the state association for your sport. District-level competition is the gateway — no district medal = no state selection = no scholarships. Don't skip this step.

NCAA basics — what Indian athletes actually need to know

The 3 divisions:
Division I — 350 schools, highest competition, full athletic scholarships allowed (in most sports). Power 5 conferences (SEC, Big Ten, ACC, Big 12, Pac-12 successor). This is the dream most Indians chase.
Division II — 300 schools, partial scholarships, more academic flex, better life-balance. Under-appreciated by Indian applicants.
Division III — 440 schools, no athletic scholarships but often generous academic/need-based aid. NESCAC (Williams, Amherst, Middlebury) = elite academics + solid sport.
NAIA + JUCO — alternatives if NCAA doesn't work. NAIA = 250 smaller schools, flexible eligibility. JUCO (2-year community colleges) = path to NCAA after 1-2 years.
NCAA Eligibility Center (mandatory first step)
Every prospective international athlete MUST register. Academic transcripts (Class 9-12), test scores (SAT/ACT — now optional at most schools but take it anyway), amateurism certification. Register in Class 11 at latest. Fee: US$150 international. Without this: no DI/DII eligibility.

Reality check on scholarships:
• "Full ride" (tuition + board + fees) is rare outside headcount sports: football, basketball (men + women), gymnastics, tennis, volleyball (women).
• Equivalency sports (track, swim, soccer, golf, baseball) split scholarships across teams — expect 25-75% of tuition, not 100%.
• D1 tennis (women): 8 scholarships / team. Very competitive, but India has historical pipeline.
• Golf: 4.5-6 scholarships / team. Very reachable for state-level Indian junior golfers.
• Track: 12.6 / team. Spread across distances + events. Indians score in distance running (5K/10K/marathon).
⚠️ Visa note: F-1 student visa. OPT gives 12 months post-study work (36 months for STEM majors). If you play a pro sport after college, you switch to P-1 (athlete) visa — possible but requires you to be among the best in your sport.
