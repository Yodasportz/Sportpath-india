NC
Neeraj Chopra
Joined a gym to lose weight at 13 → SAI found him → Olympic gold (Tokyo & World Champion). India's greatest track and field story.
AthleteAthletics
▼
📍 SAI pathway | Khandra village, Haryana
Age 13 — Panipat, Haryana
Overweight and teased by classmates, joins a local gym. Spots javelin throwers at Shivaji Stadium. Coach Jaiveer Choudhary notices his raw arm strength. Starts training with no proper equipment.
SAI system → National junior record
Enters SAI training at Tau Devi Lal Sports Complex, Panchkula. Wins National Junior Athletics Championship gold in 2012 with 68.40m — a national junior record.
2016 — World U20 Champion + Indian Army
Throws 86.48m to win gold at the IAAF World U20 Championships in Poland — India's first-ever world junior athletics title and a world record. Inducted into the Indian Army as Naib Subedar.
2021 → 2025 — Olympic gold, World Champion, 90m+
Tokyo Olympic gold (87.58m). World Champion Budapest 2023. Breaks 90m barrier at Doha Diamond League 2025. Endorsements with Gillette and Tata AIA.

"After my Olympics win, coaches reported being flooded with requests from youngsters eager to learn javelin. Stores began selling replica javelins made of bamboo."

— Neeraj Chopra
🏋 SAI spotted him — compete in AFI events to get noticed

🪖 Indian Army Sports Institute = training + income + stability

RR
Rani Rampal
Cart-puller's daughter from Shahabad → GoSports covered her kit and shoes → India hockey captain → Padma Shri.
AthleteGoSports Foundation
▼
📍 GoSports Foundation scholarship | Shahabad, Haryana
Born 1994 — Shahabad Markanda, Haryana
Father is a cart-puller, mother a domestic worker. At age 7, spots boys playing hockey near her home. Walks into the Shahabad Hockey Academy. Coach Baldev Singh initially rejects her — too slight. She returns every day until he relents.
GoSports Foundation steps in — the turning point
Family cannot afford hockey kit, shoes, or the mandatory 500ml of milk at the academy. She mixes milk with water to meet the requirement. GoSports Foundation covers her kit, equipment, and training costs. Without this, she stops playing.
2008 — International debut at age 14
Becomes the youngest player ever to represent India's women's hockey team. Scores 4 goals at the 2009 Champion's Challenge Tournament to win the event. Named top goal scorer and Young Player of the Tournament.
2010–2021 — World Cup youngest player → Olympic captain → Padma Shri
Youngest player at the 2010 World Cup (age 15). Scores 7 goals, wins Best Young Player. Captains India to their first Olympic semi-final in history (Tokyo 2020). Buys her family their first home in 2017. Padma Shri 2020.

"I wanted an escape from my life — from the electricity shortages, from barely having two meals. Papa couldn't afford to buy me a stick. But once he saw my passion, he said: play until your heart's content."

— Rani Rampal, Humans of Bombay
💚 GoSports Foundation can be life-changing for talented athletes with no money
🏑 Local academies (Shahabad HA) are real pipelines to the national team
💪 Coach Baldev Singh rejected her at first — persistence matters
🏆 A sports career can lift an entire family out of poverty

Sources: Wikipedia, Grokipedia, FirstSportz, MSN/Humans of Bombay — independently verified.

SC
Satwiksairaj & Chirag Shetty
GoSports stepped in 2–3 weeks before the Thomas Cup 2022. India won for the first time in 73 years.
AthleteGoSports Foundation
▼
📍 GoSports PACE Scholarship | Badminton doubles
GoSports PACE scholarship — early career
Both players receive GoSports Foundation PACE (Programme for Aspiring Champions in Excellence) scholarships as junior athletes. Beyond money: sports science, legal guidance on contracts, and media training — things government programmes don't cover.
2022 — The intervention that changed history
Just 2–3 weeks before the Thomas Cup 2022 in Bangkok, their coaching contract with former World No.1 Mathias Boe falls through. GoSports steps in immediately, covering the coaching costs. Without this, their preparation collapses.
India wins Thomas Cup for the first time in 73 years
Satwik and Chirag win their doubles rubbers. India defeats Indonesia 3–0 in the final. India's first Thomas Cup title since 1979. They go on to reach World No.1 in men's doubles in 2023.

"Initially, when we didn't have the contract with Mathias Boe, GoSports stepped in — it was just 2–3 weeks before the Thomas Cup 2022. Such backing helps athletes focus only on the game."

— Chirag Shetty (The Print)
💚 Private scholarships fill gaps government support leaves open
⚡ Timing of support matters as much as the amount
🤝 GoSports covers coaching, legal, science — not just cash
🏆 Scholarship → World No.1 → Thomas Cup. The pipeline is real.

Sources: The Print, MyKhel, Inlaks Foundation Blog — independently verified.
